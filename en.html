Hanafi pathway from Mumbai to Makkah — Fard, Wajib, Sunnah & Mustahabb

May Allah (SWT) accept your intention to visit His Sacred House, make the journey from Mumbai smooth, and grant you an accepted Umrah (Umrah Maqbula). This guide follows Hanafi Fiqh: Fard (compulsory), Wajib (necessary; penalty if omitted), Sunnah (Prophetic), Mustahabb (recommended).

1 Logistical Preparation & Wearing the Ihram (Mumbai)

Before leaving home, physical and spiritual preparation sets the foundation for Ihram.

  • The Ihram Garments: For men, it is highly preferred to use a highly absorbent towel-like unstitched cloth for the bottom half (Izar) and a lighter cotton or linen unstitched cloth for the top half (Rida).
  • Video Guides on How to Wear Ihram:
    • English: youtu.be/p3SgrvVjQ10
    • Hindi/Urdu: youtu.be/BgygBjDEkBg
    • Hinglish: youtu.be/IXmnpxcbHH0
  • Mustahabb Clipping nails, removing unwanted body hair, trimming the mustache.
  • Sunnah Full bath (Ghusl) with the intention of Ihram. Women in menstruation may also perform it (for physical cleanliness).
  • Mustahabb Men: apply non-alcoholic perfume (Attar) to body (head and beard) before Niyyah. Do not apply to the Ihram towels.
  • Sunnah Pray 2 Rak'ahs of Nafl Salah at home before departing (avoid Makruh times).
  • Rules for Children: If you are traveling with a young child, the parents must make the Niyyah (intention) of Ihram on their behalf.
  • Rules for Menstruating Women: If a woman is in a state of impurity, she will still assume the Ihram, but she must not pray the 2 Rak'ahs of Ihram. Upon reaching Makkah, she must simply wait at the hotel until she is pure, perform Ghusl, and then proceed to perform her Umrah.

2 The Miqat Protocol & Entering Ihram (Mid-Air)

Flights from Mumbai to Jeddah cross the Miqat (Yalamlam) about 20–30 minutes before landing. You cannot cross without being in Ihram.

  • Put on Ihram towels at CSMIA or early in the flight. Wearing the cloths alone does not put you in Ihram.
  • Fard Ihram is established only by Niyyah + Talbiyah together.
  • Crucial Warning: Do not recite the Talbiyah while boarding or waiting at the airport. The moment you combine the Niyyah and the Talbiyah, all restrictions of Ihram apply immediately.
  • Strategic Timing on the Flight: Airlines mostly announce when the Miqat is approaching. However, if you plan to eat and sleep on the flight, it is better to make your Niyyah and recite the Talbiyah before going to sleep. If you fall asleep and cross the Miqat without Ihram, a Dam (penalty sacrifice) becomes obligatory.
  • The Talbiyah: Men should recite the Talbiyah with a slightly louder voice, while women should recite it softly so only they can hear themselves.

Niyyah (Intention)

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أُرِيدُ الْعُمْرَةَ فَيَسِّرْهَا لِي وَتَقَبَّلْهَا مِنِّي

Allahumma inni uridu al-'umrata fa-yassirha li wa taqabbalha minni.

"O Allah, I intend to perform Umrah, so make it easy for me and accept it from me."

Talbiyah

Fard to recite at least once to enter Ihram; Sunnah to repeat often after.

لَبَّيْكَ اللَّهُمَّ لَبَّيْكَ، لَبَّيْكَ لاَ شَرِيكَ لَكَ لَبَّيْكَ، إِنَّ الْحَمْدَ، وَالنِّعْمَةَ، لَكَ وَالْمُلْكَ، لاَ شَرِيكَ لَكَ

Labbayk Allahumma labbayk, labbayka la sharika laka labbayk. Innal hamda, wan-ni'mata, laka wal mulk, la sharika lak.

"Here I am, O Allah, here I am. Here I am, You have no partner, here I am. Verily all praise and blessings are Yours, and all sovereignty, You have no partner."

3 The 8 Strict Restrictions of Ihram (Jinayat)

Once Niyyah and Talbiyah are made, the following restrictions apply. Violating them requires a Dam (penalty) or Sadaqah (charity):

  1. Head & Face: No covering of the head and face for men. For women, the head must be covered, but the fabric of the face veil (Niqab) must not touch the skin of the face (they can wear a cap with a visor to drape the veil). Women are allowed to wear jewelry.
  2. Fragrance: No applying of perfume, attar, or using scented soaps for both men and women.
  3. Hair & Nails: No clipping, breaking, or cutting hair from any part of the body, nor clipping nails.
  4. Clothing: Men cannot wear any stitched clothing shaped to the body (no underwear, shirts, etc.). Women wear normal, modest stitched clothing.
  5. Intimacy: Absolutely no marital activity or romantic touch for both.
  6. Footwear: Men must wear slippers/sandals that leave the instep (the top of the foot/metatarsal bone) uncovered.
  7. Flora: You cannot pluck, break, or cut grass, plants, leaves, or flowers within the boundaries of the Haram (applies to both).
  8. Fauna: No hunting. You cannot kill or harm any living creature, not even a louse in the hair or any other small insect.

4 Entering Makkah & The Haram

  • Hudood-e-Haram: While driving into Makkah, when you spot the boundary markers of the Haram (often shaped like a large Rehal or bookstand with the Quran on it), recite: "Allahumma hadha haramuka wa amnuka faharrim lahmi wa dami 'alan-nar." (O Allah, this is Your sanctuary and security, so make my flesh and blood forbidden to the Fire).
  • Resting: It is perfectly fine (and often recommended) to check into your hotel, rest, and refresh yourself before going to perform Umrah.
  • Entering Masjid al-Haram: Step in with your right foot and recite: "Allahummaf-tah li abwaba rahmatik." (O Allah, open the doors of Your mercy for me).
  • The First Glance at the Kaaba: It is a verified tradition that your Duas are accepted at the first sight of the Kaaba.
  • Strategy: Keep your gaze lowered while walking into the courtyard. Find a spot to the side where you won't block traffic and can stand for a long time. Once settled, look up at the Kaaba, raise your hands, and ask for all your needs.
  • Stop the Talbiyah: Upon seeing the Kaaba and preparing for Tawaf, you must stop reciting the Talbiyah.

5 Tawaf Mechanics

In Makkah: settle, make Wudu, go to Al-Masjid Al-Haram.

  • Sunnah Preparation (Idtiba): Men must pass the upper Ihram towel under their right armpit and drape it over the left shoulder, leaving the right shoulder completely exposed. This is only for the 7 rounds of Tawaf.
  • Alignment & Niyyah: Stand slightly before the line aligned with the Black Stone (Hajar al-Aswad), so the Kaaba is in front of you and the Stone is slightly to your right. Make the Niyyah: "Allahumma inni uridu tawafa baytikal haram fayas-sirhu li wa taqabbalhu minni." (O Allah, I intend to perform Tawaf of Your Sacred House, so make it easy for me and accept it from me). Now, step to the right so the Hajar al-Aswad is directly in front of you.
  • Sunnah Istilam: Face the Stone, raise your hands to your ears (like beginning Salah), and say "Bismillahi Allahu Akbar". Since you likely cannot kiss it, point both palms toward it as if placing your hands on the stone, and then kiss your own palms without making any smacking sound/voice.
  • Fard Seven counter-clockwise circuits.
  • Sunnah Raml (men): First three circuits — walk briskly with short steps; remaining four — normal pace.
  • The Gaze (Hanafi Ruling): Keep your gaze lowered toward the ground/your feet for Khushu (humility). Looking around or directly at the Kaaba while actively walking is considered Makruh Tanzihi (mildly disliked) because it risks turning your chest or back toward the Kaaba, which invalidates that step. Keep looking straight ahead on your path.
  • Interruptions during Tawaf: Salah: If the congregational Jama'ah begins, stop your Tawaf immediately, join the Salah where you are, and after the Salah, resume your Tawaf from the exact spot you left off. Wudu: If your Wudu breaks, stop immediately. Go out, perform Wudu, return, and resume from where you left off.

6 Post-Tawaf & Zamzam

  • Cover the Shoulder: Immediately after the 7th round, cover your right shoulder again. Idtiba is over.
  • Wajib Two Rak'ahs: Pray 2 Rak'ahs anywhere in the Haram (ideally behind Maqam Ibrahim). It is Sunnah to recite Surah Al-Kafirun in the 1st Rak'ah and Surah Al-Ikhlas in the 2nd.
  • Mustahabb Drinking Zamzam: Go to the Zamzam dispensers. Face the Kaaba. Say Bismillah, drink fully (until your stomach is full), say Alhamdulillah, and recite: "Allahumma inni as'aluka 'ilman nafi'an, wa rizqan wasi'an, wa shifa'an min kulli da'." (O Allah, I ask You for beneficial knowledge, abundant provision, and cure from every illness).
  • CRITICAL STEP (The 9th Istilam): Before proceeding to Safa for the Sa'i, it is a Sunnah to return to the Hajar al-Aswad line and perform one final (9th) Istilam.

7 Sa'i & Completion

  • Going to Safa: Proceed to the hill of Safa. Climb up to a height where you can see the Kaaba directly in front of you through the arches.
  • Niyyah for Sa'i: "Allahumma inni uridus-sa'ya baynas-safa wal marwati sab'ata ashwatin lillahi ta'ala fayas-sirhu li wa taqabbalhu minni." (O Allah, I intend to perform seven rounds of Sa'i between Safa and Marwa for the sake of Allah Ta'ala, so make it easy for me and accept it from me).
  • The Walk & Duas: Walk towards Marwa. During the walk, frequently recite the 4th Kalimah: "La ilaha illallahu wahdahu la sharika lah, lahul mulku wa lahul hamdu, wa huwa 'ala kulli shai'in qadeer." Also recite: "Rabbighfir warham, wa tajaawaz 'amma ta'lam, innaka antal a'azzul akram." (O Lord, forgive and have mercy, and overlook what You know [of my sins]; verily, You are the Most Mighty, the Most Noble).
  • At Marwa: Just as you did at Safa, turn to face the direction of the Kaaba, raise your hands, and make abundant Dua. Make Dua at both hills during every single round.
  • Rulings for Sa'i: (1) Sa'i can only be performed after completing a valid Tawaf. (2) While it is highly preferable to complete all 7 rounds continuously, if you get tired, it is completely fine to sit, take a break, and continue from where you stopped.
  • Wajib Sa'i: 7 trips between Safa and Marwa; finish at Marwa. Sunnah Green lights (men): Between the two green fluorescent lights, jog at moderate pace. Women walk normally.
  • Wajib Completion (Halq/Taqsir): Conclude your Umrah by shaving the head completely (Halq) or trimming at least one inch from one-quarter of the head (Taqsir). Women trim an inch from the bottom of their gathered hair. Your Ihram is now officially lifted. Mustahabb Halq (full shave) for men is most rewarding.

All Duas in One Place

Performing Umrah is a deeply spiritual experience, and the "tongue of the believer" is the greatest tool during this journey. In Hanafi Fiqh there are specific Duas from the Sunnah; you are also encouraged to ask for your personal needs in your own language.

1. Niyyah for Umrah (At the time of Ihram)

Recite when you are ready to enter Ihram before the Miqat.

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أُرِيدُ الْعُمْرَةَ فَيَسِّرْهَا لِي وَتَقَبَّلْهَا مِنِّي

Allahumma inni uridu al-'umrata fa-yassirha li wa taqabbalha minni.

O Allah, I intend to perform Umrah, so make it easy for me and accept it from me.

2. The Talbiyah (The Anthem of the Pilgrim)

Recite constantly after Niyyah until you reach the Kaaba.

لَبَّيْكَ اللَّهُمَّ لَبَّيْكَ، لَبَّيْكَ لاَ شَرِيكَ لَكَ لَبَّيْكَ، إِنَّ الْحَمْدَ، وَالنِّعْمَةَ، لَكَ وَالْمُلْكَ، لاَ شَرِيكَ لَكَ

Labbayk Allahumma labbayk, labbayka la sharika laka labbayk. Innal hamda, wan-ni'mata, laka wal mulk, la sharika lak.

Here I am, O Allah, here I am. Here I am, You have no partner, here I am. Verily all praise and blessings are Yours, and all sovereignty, You have no partner.

3. Entering the Haram / Makkah Boundary

Recite when you see the "Rehal" boundary markers.

اللَّهُمَّ هَذَا حَرَمُكَ وَأَمْنُكَ فَحَرِّمْ لَحْمِي وَدَمِي عَلَى النَّارِ

Allahumma hadha haramuka wa amnuka faharrim lahmi wa dami 'alan-nar.

O Allah, this is Your sanctuary and security, so make my flesh and blood forbidden to the Fire.

4. Entering Masjid al-Haram

Step in with your right foot.

بِسْمِ اللهِ وَالصَّلَاةُ وَالسَّلَامُ عَلَى رَسُولِ اللهِ، اللَّهُمَّ افْتَحْ لِي أَبْوَابَ رَحْمَتِكَ

Bismillahi was-salatu was-salamu 'ala rasulillahi, Allahummaf-tah li abwaba rahmatik.

In the name of Allah, peace and blessings be upon the Messenger of Allah. O Allah, open the doors of Your mercy for me.

5. At the First Sight of the Kaaba

There is no fixed wording; it is Sunnah to say the following, then make your personal Duas (they are accepted at this moment).

اللهُ أَكْبَرُ، لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللهُ

Allahu Akbar, La ilaha illallah.

Allah is Great, there is no god but Allah. (Follow with your personal heartfelt Duas.)

6. Starting Tawaf (At the Black Stone)

Recite at the Green Light line during Istilam.

بِسْمِ اللهِ، اللهُ أَكْبَرُ

Bismillahi, Allahu Akbar.

In the name of Allah, Allah is Great.

7. Between the Yemeni Corner and the Black Stone

Recite during the last part of every circuit of Tawaf.

رَبَّنَا آتِنَا فِي الدُّنْيَا حَسَنَةً وَفِي الْآخِرَةِ حَسَنَةً وَقِنَا عَذَابَ النَّارِ

Rabbana atina fid-dunya hasanatan wa fil-akhirati hasanatan wa qina 'adhaban-nar.

Our Lord, give us good in this world and good in the Hereafter, and protect us from the punishment of the Fire.

8. After Drinking Zamzam

Face the Kaaba and drink.

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ عِلْمًا نَافِعًا، وَرِزْقًا وَاسِعًا، وَشِفَاءً مِنْ كُلِّ دَاءٍ

Allahumma inni as'aluka 'ilman nafi'an, wa rizqan wasi'an, wa shifa'an min kulli da'.

O Allah, I ask You for beneficial knowledge, abundant provision, and a cure from every illness.

9. Niyyah for Sa'i (At Mount Safa)

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أُرِيدُ السَّعْيَ بَيْنَ الصَّفَا وَالْمَرْوَةِ سَبْعَةَ أَشْوَاطٍ لِلَّهِ تَعَالَى فَيَسِّرْهُ لِي وَتَقَبَّلْهُ مِنِّي

Allahumma inni uridus-sa'ya baynas-safa wal marwati sab'ata ashwatin lillahi ta'ala fayas-sirhu li wa taqabbalhu minni.

O Allah, I intend to perform seven rounds of Sa'i between Safa and Marwa for Your sake, so make it easy for me and accept it from me.

10. During the Walk of Sa'i (Especially between Green Lights)

رَبِّ اغْفِرْ وَارْحَمْ، وَتَجَاوَزْ عَمَّا تَعْلَمْ، إِنَّكَ أَنْتَ الْأَعَزُّ الْأَكْرَمُ

Rabbighfir warham, wa tajaawaz 'amma ta'lam, innaka antal a'azzul akram.

My Lord, forgive and have mercy, and overlook what You know (of my sins); verily You are the Most Mighty, the Most Noble.

11. General Zikr during Sa'i (The 4th Kalimah)

Recite frequently while walking between Safa and Marwa.

لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللهُ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ، لَهُ الْمُلْكُ وَلَهُ الْحَمْدُ، وَهُوَ عَلَى كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ

La ilaha illallahu wahdahu la sharika lah, lahul mulku wa lahul hamdu, wa huwa 'ala kulli shai'in qadeer.

There is no god but Allah, alone, without partner. His is the sovereignty, and His is the praise, and He has power over all things.

12. Leaving Masjid al-Haram

Step out with your left foot.

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ مِنْ فَضْلِكَ

Allahumma inni as'aluka min fadlik.

O Allah, I ask You for Your favor/bounty.

Final tip: Carry these on your phone or print them out. While these Arabic Duas are beautiful, Allah (SWT) knows the language of your heart. Don't forget to make Dua for your family, the Ummah, and your own forgiveness in your native language.

Troubleshooting: Dam (Penalty)

In Hanafi Fiqh, Dam is the obligatory sacrifice of a sheep or goat within the Haram if a pilgrim:

  1. Omits a Wajib (e.g. crossing Miqat without Ihram, skipping Sa'i without valid reason, or not praying the 2 Rak'ahs after Tawaf and then leaving Makkah).
  2. Violates an Ihram restriction (e.g. stitched clothing for a full day, or footwear covering the instep).

Missing Sunnah or Mustahabb carries no penalty (reward is less). If a Fard is missed (e.g. Tawaf), the Umrah is invalid and cannot be fixed by sacrifice.
